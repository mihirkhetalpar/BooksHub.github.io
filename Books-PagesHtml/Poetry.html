Flowers of Evil

Author: Charles Baudelaire

Genre: Poetry, Symbolism

Published: 1857

Summary

"Flowers of Evil" is a groundbreaking collection of poetry by Charles Baudelaire. This influential work explores themes of beauty, decadence, and the complexities of human nature. Baudelaire's evocative and sometimes controversial poems delve into the dark and sublime, offering a unique perspective on the human experience and emotions.

Jacob's Room

Author: Virginia Woolf

Genre: Fiction, Modernism

Published: 1922

Summary

"Jacob's Room" is a novel by Virginia Woolf that reflects her innovative narrative style and exploration of human consciousness. The story follows Jacob Flanders, offering fragmented glimpses into his life, relationships, and the impact of World War I. Woolf's experimental prose and vivid character sketches create a captivating and introspective narrative, revealing the complexities of human existence.

The Book of American Negro Poetry

Author: Edited by James Weldon Johnson

Genre: Poetry Anthology

Published: 1922

Summary

"The Book of American Negro Poetry" edited by James Weldon Johnson is a significant anthology that showcases the rich heritage of African American poetry. It features works by various poets, providing insights into their experiences, struggles, and cultural expressions. This collection offers a diverse range of poetic voices, highlighting the profound impact of African American poets on literary traditions.

The Madman

Author: Kahlil Gibran

Genre: Poetry

Published: 1918

Summary

"The Madman" by Kahlil Gibran is a collection of poetic parables and reflections that delve into various aspects of human nature and spirituality. Through thought-provoking verses, Gibran explores themes of love, freedom, self-discovery, and the human condition. His profound insights and philosophical musings offer readers a journey into the depths of introspection and contemplation.

Twas the Night Before Christmas

Author: Clement Clarke Moore

Genre: Poetry, Children's Literature

Published: 1823

Summary

"Twas the Night Before Christmas," also known as "A Visit from St. Nicholas," is a beloved and iconic poem by Clement Clarke Moore. First published anonymously in 1823, this classic Christmas poem tells the story of a visit from St. Nicholas on Christmas Eve. The poem captures the magic and joy of the holiday season, describing St. Nicholas' arrival, his reindeer, and his jolly demeanor as he brings gifts to children.
